Evidence Level

Evidence level reports proof depth independently from execution mode: minimal runtime, certified, or full replay.

CategoryPerformance Architecture Evidence fields4 Reference files2
Meaning Why it matters How used Support Evidence Claim boundary Sources

Plain-English meaning

Evidence level reports proof depth independently from execution mode: minimal runtime, certified, or full replay.

Why it matters

This concept helps users interpret ShardLoom's performance architecture posture without reading the phase plan or RFCs first.

How ShardLoom uses it

ShardLoom surfaces this through docs, capability/status pages, use cases, or evidence fields such as evidence_level, claim_gate_status, fallback_attempted, external_engine_invoked.

Current support

Current status is current-vocabulary. Treat that status exactly as shown; scoped, smoke, report-only, planned, blocked, or unsupported posture must not be read as broader runtime support.

Evidence fields

It helps identify the exact evidence or documentation surface that applies to this concept.

  • claim_gate_status
  • fallback_attempted
  • external_engine_invoked

What it does not claim

Evidence level does not silently upgrade claim status.
